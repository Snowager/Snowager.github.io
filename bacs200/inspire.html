Captain America

Captain America may not be real, but the principles and intentions behind his actions are real enough to make him a hero to me. Captain America stands for a conceptual justice, much like Superman, where good always wins in the end. the difference between them, however, is that Superman is an almost invincible alien being whereas Captain America is just a supersoldier whose only power is his unbreakable conviction. It's the fact that even with far more to lose, both physical and emotionally, Captain America continues to fight for the powers of good that makes him my hero.

Some of the heroic actions that Captain America has done in the past include:

  • Working tirelessly to save his friend Bucky Barnes.
  • Helping to defeat Thanos and protect the Earth.
  • And the best one, being deemed worthy enough to wield Mjolnir, Thor's Hammer that can only be used by those pure of heart and noble in their intentions.

Despite being a figure of fiction, Captain America's realistic ability to keep hope no matter the odds makes him a consistent source of inspiration for me. To always fight the good fight, against oppression, against hatred, against tyranny, and against all odds.
